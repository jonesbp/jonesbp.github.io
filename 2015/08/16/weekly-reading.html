Weekly Reading: August 16, 2015

16 Aug 2015

Links

  • Facebook Should Pay All of Us—TIm Wu—The New Yorker: This is just a short bit and there’s not a ton new here if you’re at all familiar with conversations about personal data, privacy, and commerce on the internet. I link to it because I’d never seen anyone invoke the concept of “arbitrage” to describe the business model of taking undervalued personal information from customers and holding it for a future valuable purpose, but of course that’s what it is…

Books

  • Margalit Fox. The Riddle of the Labyrinth: The Quest to Crack an Ancient Code. New York: Ecco, 2013.

    Margalit Fox is the obituaries editor for the New York Times, and it seems clear to me that the marks of that occupation are all over this account. I was struck repeatedly by the different decisions she made than I would have as a historian; the relative freedom she had to be impressionistic with regard to chronology and causal relationships in order perhaps to reach a truer picture of individual. I thoroughly enjoyed the narrative and appreciated that she did not shy away from a serious, but accessible, engagement with some of the deciphering practices developed by her subjects.
